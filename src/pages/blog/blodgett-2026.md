## A New Era for the Northwest Flank

For years, the northwestern corridor of Colorado Springs has served as a critical access point for local riders, but the fragmented trail network limited true back-country connectivity. 

With the approval and initial groundbreaking of the **Blodgett 2026 Expansion**, Arkose Collective and our community partners are taking a massive step toward finalizing the missing links in the 63-mile Ring the Peak epic.

> "This isn't just about adding a few miles of dirt. It's about laying the foundational bedrock that connects the urban core directly to the high alpine. It changes everything."

### The Bedrock Logic

The new alignments prioritize:
1. **Sustainable Grades:** Reducing erosion while maximizing flow for mountain bikes.
2. **Multi-Modal Access:** Ensuring riders can access the trailheads without needing a vehicle (The Carless Afternoon initiative).
3. **Strategic Pairings:** Connecting the new descending lines directly into the commercial corridors of local breweries and bike shops.

### What's Next?

Phase 1 trail crews are already cutting the lower loops. We anticipate the primary climbing access route to open by mid-summer, with the technical descending lines to follow.

Stay tuned to this space. We will be organizing volunteer dig days and strategic planning sessions as the season progresses. The bedrock is being laid.
